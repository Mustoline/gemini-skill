# Buzzword Correction Guide

This reference provides concrete, ROI-focused alternatives to common abstract business and digital buzzwords. Use these to ensure our copy feels grounded and pragmatic for the Danish market.

## 1. Abstract Business Buzzwords

| Buzzword | Concrete Alternative | Why it's better |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **"Synergy"** | "Operational alignment" or "Cross-departmental efficiency" | Explains *how* value is created. |
| **"Disruption"** | "Market transformation" or "New competitive advantage" | Focuses on the business outcome. |
| **"Leverage"** | "Utilize," "Harness," or "Scale" | "Leverage" is often overused and sounds like jargon. |
| **"Thought Leadership"** | "Expertise-driven insight" or "Strategic perspective" | Sounds less self-important and more value-driven. |
| **"Holistic"** | "End-to-end" or "Systemic" | "Holistic" often feels vague; "end-to-end" implies process. |
| **"Game-changer"** | "High-impact feature" or "Key differentiator" | Quantifies the benefit. |

## 2. Digital Industry Buzzwords

| Buzzword | Concrete Alternative | Why it's better |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **"User-centric"** | "Designed for [Specific Persona]" | Shows we actually know who the user is. |
| **"Seamless"** | "Frictionless" or "Automated" | Explains the *experience* or *technical* reason. |
| **"Omnichannel"** | "Consistent cross-platform experience" | More descriptive of the actual goal. |
| **"Big Data"** | "Behavioral insights" or "Dataset analysis" | "Big Data" is too broad; specify what the data does. |
| **"Digital Transformation"** | "Modernizing legacy workflows" or "Process automation" | Defines the actual *work* being done. |
| **"AI-powered"** | "Enhanced with predictive analysis" or "Automated recommendation" | Be specific about the AI's function. |

## 3. Review Principles
- **Be Specific**: Instead of "improve performance," say "reduce load times by 40%."
- **Focus on Action**: Use active verbs. Instead of "facilitating success," say "driving growth."
- **Avoid Hype**: If a word sounds like it belongs in a generic SaaS commercial, replace it with a pragmatic business term.
